Lost in Time

"Lost in Time" follows the journey of Sarah, a brilliant scientist who accidentally invents a time-travel device and finds herself trapped in a perilous loop through various historical eras. As she desperately seeks a way back home, she uncovers a hidden conspiracy that threatens not only her existence but the fabric of time itself. Racing against time, Sarah must unravel the truth and find a way to restore the delicate balance of the temporal continuum.

  • Rating: 6.8/10
  • Length: 132 min
  • Premiere: 15 October 2023 (USA)
  • Category: Science Fiction/Adventure/Mystery/Time Travel/Thriller
  • Director: Emma Harper
  • MPAA: Rated PG-13 for for intense sequences of action, some violence, brief language

Featured Reviews:

Fell Asleep - ☆☆☆☆ - Shii, I fell asleep during 'Lost in Time.' This sci-fi flick had all the ingredients for an exciting time travel adventure, but it failed to keep me engaged and invested. The film boasts some visually stunning historical settings and a few cool action sequences, I'll give it that. But man, the story was all over the place. I couldn't make sense of half the things that were happening on screen. It felt like they were trying to cram too many ideas into one movie, and it ended up being a confusing mess. The characters didn't do much to grab my attention either. They were forgettable and lacked depth. I didn't feel any connection to them or care about their journey. It's like they were just going through the motions, and I couldn't bring myself to care about anything happening to them. The pacing was a major issue too. It dragged on and on, and I found myself dozing off multiple times. Maybe it was just me, but I couldn't stay awake during some of the slower parts. It felt like the film could have been trimmed down to make it more concise and engaging. Overall, 'Lost in Time' left me feeling indifferent. It had potential, but it squandered it with a convoluted plot, lackluster characters, and pacing that put me to sleep. If you're a hardcore sci-fi enthusiast, you might find something to enjoy here. But for me, it was a disappointing experience that I won't be revisiting.

Pretty Good - ☆☆☆☆☆☆☆ - Okay, I'll be honest. 'Lost in Time' unexpectedly got to me, and yes, I shed a tear or two. Now, before you roll your eyes, hear me out. This film had a way of tapping into emotions I didn't anticipate. The story revolved around love, loss, and the complexities of human connections across time. The characters were flawed but relatable, and their struggles resonated with me on a personal level. There was a particular scene—a heart-wrenching moment of farewell between two characters—that hit me like a ton of bricks. The raw emotion conveyed by the actors, coupled with a hauntingly beautiful score, had me on the verge of tears. It was a testament to the power of storytelling and its ability to evoke genuine emotional responses. Sure, 'Lost in Time' had its flaws. The pacing felt uneven at times, and certain plot threads could have been better developed. But these shortcomings didn't outweigh the emotional impact it had on me. Sometimes, a film doesn't need to be perfect to touch your heart. It's about the moments that strike a chord, that make you reflect on your own life and relationships. 'Lost in Time' managed to do just that, reminding me of the power of love and the transient nature of time. If you're open to experiencing a mix of emotions and don't mind forgiving a few narrative missteps, give 'Lost in Time' a chance. It might surprise you, making you reach for a tissue and leaving you with a lingering sense of nostalgia. Just be prepared for a rollercoaster of emotions that could take you from laughter to tears in the blink of an eye.
